Overview

Keeping Tenants Happy, Safe & Compliant

Craig Kershaw Ltd has been delivering accessibility adaptations for social housing providers and private homeowners across Lancashire for over three decades. Our work helps tenants live independently, improves property compliance, and extends the usable life of housing stock.

From wet room conversions and wheelchair ramps to full door widening and ground-floor extensions, we handle every aspect of the adaptation with sensitivity, speed and professionalism. We understand the regulations and work closely with occupational therapists and housing officers to ensure every adaptation meets the tenant's specific needs.

What We Deliver

Adaptation Capabilities

A full range of accessibility adaptations designed to improve independence, safety and quality of life.

Wet Rooms

Safe, accessible wet rooms designed for wheelchair users and those with limited mobility. Level-access showering with non-slip flooring and grab rails throughout.

Extensions

Extra room for accessible living. Ground-floor bedroom and bathroom extensions that allow tenants to remain in their homes as their needs change.

Door Widening

Accommodate wheelchairs and walkers by widening internal and external doorways. Structural alterations carried out to building regulations with minimal disruption.

Front Door Adaptations

Safe and accessible entry solutions including level thresholds, wider doorframes and automated door openers to improve independence at the front of the property.

Accessibility Ramps

Safe property access with properly graded ramps built to regulations. Concrete, block-paved and modular options available to suit the property and budget.

External Doors

Seamless wheelchair access with wider, low-threshold external doors. Designed for easy operation while maintaining security and weather resistance.

Common Questions

Accessibility Adaptations FAQ

Do you understand the regulations for accessibility adaptations? +
Yes. We have over 30 years of experience working with social housing providers and local authorities on accessibility projects. We are fully familiar with Part M building regulations, DDA requirements and the Disabled Facilities Grant process. We work closely with occupational therapists to ensure every adaptation meets the specific needs identified in assessments.
Can you respond to urgent adaptation requests? +
Yes. We understand that some adaptations are time-critical — for example, when a tenant is due to be discharged from hospital. We can fast-track urgent works and have the capacity to mobilise quickly when needed. Speak to us about your timescales and we will do our best to accommodate.
How do you manage timelines and budgets? +
Every project begins with a detailed survey and fully itemised quote. We agree timelines upfront and keep you informed throughout. Our pricing is transparent with no hidden costs. For housing providers managing multiple properties, we offer streamlined scheduling and batch pricing to maximise efficiency and value.
How do you ensure tenant safety during works? +
Tenant safety is our top priority. We carry out full risk assessments before starting work, maintain clear access routes at all times, and ensure temporary facilities are available when bathrooms or kitchens are being adapted. Our team is DBS checked and trained to work sensitively in occupied homes, particularly with vulnerable tenants.
