Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical house extension take?
+
A single-storey extension typically takes 10-14 weeks from groundwork to completion, while a double-storey extension may take 14-20 weeks. Timescales depend on the size and complexity of the project, planning requirements, and weather conditions. We'll give you a realistic timeline during your free consultation.
Do I need planning permission for an extension?
+
Many single-storey rear extensions fall under Permitted Development rights, meaning you may not need full planning permission. However, this depends on the size, location, and type of property. We handle all planning applications and building regulation submissions on your behalf, so you don't have to worry about the paperwork.
Can you work on my home while I'm still living in it?
+
Absolutely. The vast majority of our residential projects are completed while homeowners continue to live in their property. Our team is experienced in minimising disruption — we keep work areas tidy, protect your furnishings, and communicate clearly about what to expect each day.
What's included in your quote?
+
Our quotes are fully transparent and itemised. They include all labour, materials, skip hire, scaffolding (if required), and any specialist work such as plumbing or electrics. The price we quote is the price you pay — no hidden costs, no surprises. We also handle building regulation fees and can advise on planning costs.
